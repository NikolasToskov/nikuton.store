What I Learned That Night About Heel Pain & Compression

That evening, I fell down a rabbit hole researching plantar fasciitis and compression therapy. For the first time, things actually made sense.

Most heel and arch pain — especially plantar fasciitis — comes from a combination of:

  • Inflammation in the plantar fascia (the band along the bottom of your foot)
  • Poor circulation to the damaged tissue
  • Instability and overstretching every time your heel hits the ground

That’s why doctors often recommend compression as part of the classic R.I.C.E. protocol: Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation.

Compression does three simple but powerful things:

  • Boosts circulation so more oxygen & nutrients reach inflamed tissue.
  • Reduces swelling, which means less pressure and less pain.
  • Supports the arch & heel so the plantar fascia isn’t constantly overstretched.

The problem? Most compression socks just squeeze the leg. They don’t target the arch, heel and ankle the way real foot-pain sufferers need.
